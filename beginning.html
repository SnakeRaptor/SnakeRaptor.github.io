About Me

The Beginning

I was born on the 21st of August, 2003. The day of my birth wasn't very interesting, but the year had quite a few major events. You can read a little about them down below.

Last Signal from Pioneer 10

On January 23rd, the last signals were received from NASA's Pioneer 10 spacecraft. Pioneer 10 was a space probe launched in 1972 and was humanity's first mission to Jupiter. After completing its original mission, it became the first man-made object to achieve escape velocity out of the solar system.

Read more about Pioneer 10 here.

The Columbia Disaster

On the 1st of February, the NASA space shuttle Columbia broke up while re-entering the Earth's atmosphere killing all seven members of the crew. Investigations found that a section of the shuttle's thermal protection system, on the left wing, had been damaged by debris from the external fuel tank during take-off. This allowed heat produced by re-entry to enter, and destroy, the shuttle's wing structure.

Read more about the Columbia Disaster here.

Iraq War Begins

On March 20th, a coalition of the United States, United Kingdom, Australia and Poland launched their invasion of Iraq starting with a major bombing campaign. While the coalition claimed Iraq possessed weapons of mass destruction, in order to justify the war, no evidence of any such weapons was found.

Read more about the Iraq War here.

2003 Treaty of Accession

On the 16th of April, ten countries signed a treaty to join the European Union. The countries were: Czech Republic, Cyprus, Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania, Hungary, Malta, Poland, Slovakia and Slovenia. This represented one of the largest expansions of the European Union since its creation.

Read more about the Treaty of Accession here.

2003 Eurovision Song Contest

Just like (almost) every other year, a Eurovision Song Contest was held in 2003. It was held in Latvia in May that year, with Turkey winning with the song "Everyway That I Can" by Sertab Erener and Demir Demirkan.

Read more about the Eurovision Song Contest here.

Last Commercial Concorde Flight

On the 24th of October, the final commercial flight of Concorde touched down at London Heathrow airport bringing the age of supersonic air travel to an end. While a sad time for aviation enthusiasts, the aircraft was nearly fourty years old and struggled to keep up with the luxury offered by larger subsonic aircraft. In addition, Airbus made the decision to stop supplying spare parts for the aircraft which was really the final nail in the coffin.

Read more about Concorde here.
